What is Economic Inequality?

Why is Economic Inequality a problem today, in the United States?

How has it evolved over the last several decades?

Economic Inequality

Economic inequality is the difference found in various measures of economic well-being among individuals in a group, among groups in a population, or among countries.

Over the last several decades, economic inequality between weaalthiest 1% and the rest of the United States has grown to alarming rates. It has fueled domestic social issues and increased the fragility of our economy.

The New Deal was a radical collection of economic policies, intended to fix the problems of the Great Depression and prevent it from happening again.

Through increased government regulations placed on businesses and the safety net established by Social Security. Along with the extreme increase of military spending during WWII, the amount of wealth owned by the 1% dropped around 20%.

Source: Wealth Inequality in the United States since 1913: Evidence from Capitalized Income Tax Data, Emmanuel Saez and Gabriel Zucman, August 2015
